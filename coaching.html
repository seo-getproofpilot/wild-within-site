You're not in crisis. You're not falling apart. You're functioning, even doing well by most measures. And you also know — quietly, persistently — that there's more.

More clarity. More courage. More direction. More aliveness. A version of you that you can almost see but haven't been able to step into.

A note on what coaching is not: Coaching is not a substitute for therapy. If you're struggling with depression, anxiety, trauma, or any other mental health concern, that's therapy work. We won't try to make coaching do something it isn't designed to do.

Know The Difference

Coaching vs. therapy — which do you need?

Both are valuable. They serve different purposes. Here's how to tell.

Therapy

  • → Healing — looks backward and inward
  • → What shaped you, what you're carrying
  • → Clinical, regulated, licensed
  • → Treats mental health conditions

Coaching

  • → Growth — looks forward and outward
  • → Where you're going, what you want to build
  • → Goals, accountability, action
  • → Available nationwide (not state-licensed)

Not sure which you need? That's what the free consultation is for. Some people need both, in sequence or in parallel.

What coaching actually looks like

People hear "life coaching" and sometimes picture vision boards and motivational quotes. That's not what this is. Coaching with me is practical, specific, and rooted in the real stuff you're navigating right now.

We might spend a session mapping out the career move you've been afraid to make, breaking it into actual steps instead of letting it live as this big scary thing in the back of your head. We might work on the conversation you've been avoiding with your partner, your boss, or your parent, and figure out what you actually want to say and why it feels so hard to say it. We might dig into why you keep setting goals and not following through, not because you're lazy but because something underneath is getting in the way. We might design a morning practice that actually sticks because it's built around your real life, not some idealized version of it.

Every session is different because every client is different. But the thread is the same: we figure out what's in the way, we make a plan, and then I hold you to it. Not in a drill-sergeant way. In a "you told me this mattered to you, so let's make sure you're actually doing it" way. With warmth. And occasionally with the kind of honesty your friends won't give you.

Built for forward motion

Coaching looks ahead. It's about the life you're building, the decisions you're making, and the version of yourself you're stepping into. With someone grounded enough to hold you accountable and skilled enough to know when the real work is deeper than strategy.

Common Questions

FAQ

What's the difference between a coach and a therapist?

Therapists are licensed mental health professionals who treat mental health conditions and work with the past. Coaches focus on goals, growth, and the future. I am both — when you book coaching, we're doing coaching work, not therapy.

How much does coaching cost?

Coaching is priced separately from therapy. Reach out for current pricing — packages and single sessions both available.

How long does a coaching engagement last?

It depends on what you're working on. Some people come for a focused 8–12 week sprint around a specific goal. Others stay longer for ongoing growth. We design the engagement around your needs.

Do I need a coach or a therapist?

If you're working through depression, anxiety, trauma, grief, relationship struggles tied to your past, or any mental health concern — therapy. If you're functioning well and want growth, accountability, and forward movement — coaching. Sometimes the answer is both.

Can I do both with you?

I can offer either, but not both at the same time with the same person. If we start in therapy and you transition into coaching (or vice versa), we'll talk through it carefully. Sometimes the clearer path is to do one with me and the other with someone else.

Does insurance cover coaching?

No. Coaching is not a clinical service and insurance reimbursement is not available for coaching sessions. No diagnosis is offered through coaching. If you need a clinical diagnosis or insurance-reimbursable treatment, that falls under therapy.
