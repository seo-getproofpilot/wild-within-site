Tantra Coaching · Online Worldwide

Sacred sexuality, embodiment, and the deeper kind of intimacy.

Held by a licensed clinical therapist who is also a certified tantra practitioner. A rare combination, on purpose.

Most of what people think they know about tantra is wrong.

Tantra is not a sex technique. It is not a religion. It is not weekend retreats with strangers and incense. It is not the Sting joke.

Tantra is a path. A way of life. A practice of being in sacred relationship with yourself, with the people you love, and with the experience of being alive in a body. At its core, it teaches us to come into deep, conscious, embodied presence with what is — including the parts of being human that most of us were taught to be ashamed of.

Most of us were raised with a complicated, often painful relationship to sex and sexuality. Maybe our parents never talked about it. Maybe the messaging was unhealthy or shaming. Maybe there was trauma. Tantra offers a path back — toward sex and intimacy as something sacred, alive, connective, and yours.

Why someone with my background, doing this work

This is the part most tantra coach websites can't say.

I'm a licensed clinical social worker (LCSW) with years of trauma training, EMDR, parts work, and clinical experience. I'm also a certified tantra practitioner with training in meditation, couples work, and sacred sexuality. I'm currently completing tantra yoga teacher certification.

That combination is rare. Most tantra coaches don't have clinical training. Most clinicians won't go anywhere near this material. I bring both. That means we can do real embodied tantric work in a container that is also psychologically safe, trauma-informed, and grounded in actual professional ethics.

If you've been curious about tantra but skeptical about whether it's legitimate, or worried about safety, or unsure whether the person teaching it knows what they're doing — that's exactly why I built this part of my practice.

Who tantra coaching is for

  • Individuals who want a deeper, more embodied relationship with their own pleasure, body, and sexuality
  • Couples wanting to deepen intimacy beyond the physical
  • People healing the shame, fear, or trauma they carry around sex and sexuality
  • People who feel disconnected from their own desire and want to find their way back
  • Anyone who has been curious about tantra and wanted a safe, professional, grounded guide
  • People navigating identity, sensuality, and authentic expression

A note on scope: Tantra coaching is coaching, not therapy. If you are working through clinically significant trauma, that may need to happen in therapy first or in parallel with another provider. We'll talk about it during your consultation and figure out what's right.

How online sessions work

All tantra coaching sessions are virtual and held over secure video. You can join from anywhere in the world. Sessions are typically 60–90 minutes depending on the work. Some clients book single sessions; others work with me over a series of weeks or months on a deeper container.

Sessions are conversational, embodied, and often include guided practice you can do in your own space — breathwork, somatic awareness, intention work, communication exercises if you're working as a couple. Nothing physical happens between us. This is coaching, held with the same ethics and boundaries as any clinical work.

FAQ

What is tantra coaching?

Tantra coaching is the application of tantric principles — embodiment, presence, sacred relationship, conscious intimacy — to your real life. It can be focused on individual embodiment, on a couple's intimacy, or on healing your relationship with sexuality.

Is this the same as sex therapy?

No. Sex therapy is a clinical treatment for specific sexual dysfunction. Tantra coaching is an embodied growth practice rooted in spiritual and somatic tradition. The two can complement each other but they are different.

What happens in a session?

Conversation, somatic and breath practice, intention work, guided embodiment exercises, and (for couples) communication and connection practice. Everything happens with you in your own space — there is no physical contact between practitioner and client.

Is tantra religious?

No. Tantra has roots in spiritual tradition, but it is not a religion. People of any faith (or none) can practice it. We focus on the practical, embodied, relational dimensions.

Can this help my relationship?

Yes — many couples come to tantra coaching to deepen intimacy beyond the surface. If your relationship is in active crisis, couples therapy may be a better starting point. We can talk it through.

How do online sessions work?

Sessions are held over secure video. You'll need a quiet, private space and a stable internet connection. Couples can join together from one location.

Is this legitimate?

Yes. I am a licensed clinical social worker, a trauma-trained therapist, and a certified tantra practitioner. This work is held with full professional ethics, including clear scope, boundaries, and informed consent.

Do I need to have a partner?

No. Most of my tantra clients are individuals doing their own embodiment and reclamation work. Couples work is offered too, but partnership is not a requirement.
