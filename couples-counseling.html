Couples Counseling · Mesa, AZ

It's not too late.

Couples counseling for the people who love each other and are tired of the same fight.

✦

You're probably here because something has been off for a while. Maybe a long while.

Maybe you've stopped really talking. Maybe the fights end the same way every time. Maybe trust took a hit and you're not sure how to come back from it. Maybe a baby, or a job loss, or a move, or a death in the family put space between you that you don't know how to close. Maybe you still love each other — and you can't figure out why that's not enough.

Here's what I want you to know before we even meet: it's almost never too late. The research says couples wait an average of six years longer than they should before reaching out. If you're here at all, you're already ahead of most people. That's the brave part.

You still love each other. That matters.

Most couples who walk through my door haven't stopped caring. They've stopped knowing how to show it in a way the other person can feel. The distance, the fights, the silence — those aren't signs the relationship is broken. They're signs you need a new language together.

Couples counseling gives you that language, and a guide who won't take sides while you learn it.

Sound Familiar?

Why couples come in.

Some of the most common reasons:

Communication has broken down. The same fight keeps happening on a loop.

Trust has been damaged — by an affair, a lie, a slow drift, or a decade of small unkept promises.

You've grown apart. You don't know each other the way you used to.

A big life change (a baby, a move, a loss) created distance you can't close on your own.

Intimacy has changed — emotionally, physically, both — and you're not sure how to talk about it.

You love each other and you keep hurting each other and you can't figure out the pattern.

You're doing fine, and you want to do better. (Yes — that's a great reason to come in too.)

My Approach

Learning a new language together.

"You're learning to speak something neither of you grew up hearing."

— Alicia

I draw heavily from Relational Life Therapy (RLT), parts work, and practical regulation tools. The goal isn't to figure out who's right. It's to help both of you understand the dance you've been doing — often without knowing — and learn a new language together.

We learn how to shift from conflict that hurts into conflict that creates intimacy, growth, and bonding. Yes — conflict can do that. When you learn how.

A safe space for both of you

My office isn't a courtroom. Nobody is on trial. Couples counseling works when both people feel safe enough to be honest — about what hurts, what they need, and what they're afraid to ask for.

I create that safety by holding space for both sides, calling out patterns without blame, and teaching you how to repair instead of retreat. The goal isn't to win the argument. It's to understand the person across from you.

Who I work with

Married couples, dating couples, engaged couples, long-term partners, blended families. Couples in their twenties and couples in their sixties. People who got here because of one big rupture and people who got here because of a thousand small ones. I do not require you to be married. I do not require you to be on the same page about why you're coming in. I just need you both to show up willing.

Common Questions

FAQ

Does couples counseling actually work?

Yes — when both people show up willing. The biggest predictor of success isn't how bad things are, it's whether both partners are open to the work. I've seen couples come back from things they thought were unfixable, and I've watched couples who looked fine on paper realize they wanted very different things. Either way, the clarity is the win.

When should we start couples counseling?

As soon as the same fight keeps happening, or as soon as you feel distance growing, or as soon as one of you starts wondering whether to stay. You do not have to be in crisis. Some of my best work is with couples who came in to make a good thing better.

What if my partner doesn't want to come?

Start individually. Doing your own work often shifts the dynamic in ways that change your partner's mind. And if it doesn't — you'll still have done the work, which matters either way.

How much does couples counseling cost?

Sessions are $185. The Wild Within is out-of-network and provides superbills you can submit for possible insurance reimbursement.

How long does couples counseling take?

It depends on what you're working on. Some couples come for a few months around a specific issue. Others stay longer for deeper relational work. We talk openly about pacing throughout.

Can we do telehealth couples sessions?

Yes. Couples sessions are available in-person at our Mesa office or via secure telehealth anywhere in Arizona.
